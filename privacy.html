Privacy Policy

Last Updated: April 5, 2026

Version Notice: Rollapaya support documentation is version-aware. Privacy disclosures differ between versions 1.1.1 and below and versions 1.2 and above, especially for advertising behavior.

Welcome to Rollapaya. This privacy policy explains how Matthew Fadoul ("I", "me", or "the Developer") handles data within the Rollapaya iOS application.

1. Overview

Rollapaya is designed to be a privacy-focused utility. I do not require user accounts, and I do not collect or store your personal identity, name, or contact information on any private servers.

2. Data Collection and Usage

App Functionality: Rollapaya stores app settings, saved configurations, and lightweight app state locally on your device, and some of that information may also sync through your personal iCloud account if your Apple device settings allow it. I do not have direct access to that locally stored information.

The app uses local device storage for functionality such as preferences, dice configuration state, and similar in-app convenience features. This site and the app do not require you to create a Rollapaya account.

3. Third-Party Advertising (Google AdMob)

Versions 1.1.1 and Below

Rollapaya includes Google AdMob-related advertising infrastructure. However, versions 1.1.1 and below do not currently serve live ads. Those versions also do not request App Tracking Transparency (ATT) permission and do not rely on IDFA-based personalized advertising.

Because Google AdMob code is included in the app, Google’s services and policies remain relevant to the app’s advertising setup. More information is available in Google's Privacy Policy.

Versions 1.2 and Above

Versions 1.2 and above use version-specific ad behavior:

  • Simulator builds: placeholder ad surfaces only; no live ads are requested or shown.
  • Debug builds on physical devices: Google AdMob test configuration may be used so that test ads can be requested during development and QA.
  • Release and App Store builds: production AdMob app IDs and banner ad unit IDs may be used so that real ads can be requested and displayed.

For versions 1.2 and above, the current disclosure plan remains non-tracking advertising. Rollapaya does not currently request ATT permission, does not use IDFA-based personalized advertising, and does not intentionally track your activity across other companies' apps or websites unless that product decision changes in a future release.

When AdMob is active, Google's SDK and related services may process data needed to deliver ads, prevent fraud, measure performance, and support diagnostics. In accordance with Apple's Privacy Labels, the following categories may be collected by the AdMob SDK:

  • Identifiers: Device ID.
  • Usage Data: Product interactions and advertising data.
  • Diagnostics: Crash logs and performance metrics to ensure stability.
  • Location: Coarse or approximate location derived from IP address.

If advertising behavior or related data practices change in a future release, this policy will be updated before or with that release.

4. App Tracking Transparency (ATT)

Across both version ranges covered by this page, Rollapaya does not currently request ATT permission, and the app does not currently track your activity across other apps or websites. If that changes in a future release, this policy will be updated before or with that release.

5. GDPR and International Rights

For users in the European Economic Area (EEA) and the UK, versions that use advertising-related features may use a Consent Management Platform (UMP) to support applicable consent requirements. Where consent choices are presented in-app, you have the right to withdraw your consent at any time via the available in-app settings.

6. Children's Privacy

Rollapaya does not knowingly collect data from children under the age of 13. AdMob is configured to respect "Tag for Under Age of Consent" (TFUA) settings where applicable.
